# Boundary Policy

The Hidden Equations of Feeling is a symbolic formula atlas and creative design-pattern tool.

It exists to help people think about comedy, drama, emotion, story structure, NPC behavior, education, and reflective prompts in a memorable way.

It is not a clinical, diagnostic, empirical, universal, predictive, or human-score system.

## Core boundary statement

> These formulas are symbolic design equations and creative metaphors only. They are not clinical tools, diagnostic models, empirical laws, universal claims, or scores of a person.

This boundary should appear in the README, app interface, manuscript, release notes, and any public educational material.

## Allowed uses

The formulas may be used for:

- comedy writing
- drama writing
- storytelling
- game design
- NPC design
- reflective journaling prompts
- classroom or workshop discussion
- creative research
- pattern recognition
- playful infographic design
- humane interface design

## Disallowed framing

Do not frame the formulas as:

- medical advice
- therapy
- diagnosis
- psychological testing
- emotional scoring
- personality scoring
- empirical prediction
- universal law
- proof of a scientific theory
- proof of a metaphysical belief
- a tool to manipulate people
- a way to shame, rank, or judge people

## Sensitive emotion rules

Extra care is required for formulas involving:

- grief
- shame
- fear
- anxiety
- anger
- trauma-adjacent material
- love and attachment
- family conflict
- spiritual or religious conditioning
- identity, belonging, exclusion, or humiliation

These cards must include:

- a boundary note
- a misuse warning
- a gentle reflection prompt
- non-shaming language
- no diagnosis
- no pressure to forgive, reconcile, calm down, or feel better

## Comedy boundary

Comedy cards may be sharp, absurd, and silly, but they should not depend on cruelty toward vulnerable people.

A good comedy formula punches at patterns, systems, absurdity, hypocrisy, ego, confusion, or format collapse — not at people who are already harmed or marginalized.

## Drama boundary

Drama cards may describe tension, conflict, overreaction, suspicion, or escalation. They should not be used to mock real suffering or dismiss valid emotions.

The goal is to understand patterns, not shame intensity.

## Reflection boundary

Reflection prompts are optional invitations, not prescriptions.

A user should never feel forced to disclose, forgive, repair, calm down, or reframe pain before they are ready.

## Contributor review questions

Before accepting a new formula, ask:

1. Is this clearly symbolic and non-diagnostic?
2. Could this be misused to shame, rank, or manipulate someone?
3. Does the formula include buffers, repair, support, or context where needed?
4. Does it respect cultural and personal variation?
5. Does it avoid claiming more than it can support?
6. Is the reflection prompt gentle and consent-based?
7. Does the misuse warning name the main risk clearly?

## Public release rule

No release should be tagged until the core boundary appears in:

- README
- app UI
- manuscript
- release notes
- Zenodo description
- relevant docs

## Short public-safe phrasing

Use this when space is limited:

> Symbolic design patterns only — not diagnosis, measurement, or universal law.

## PHI369 / Professor Phi phrasing

Use this when writing in project voice:

> Wonder without overclaiming. Feeling without scoring. Pattern without shame.
