# Formula Family Guide

This guide explains the purpose, tone, examples, and boundary style for each formula family in **The Hidden Equations of Feeling**.

Formula families help users browse the atlas, understand emotional/story patterns, and build new cards without losing the project boundary.

## Core boundary

Every family shares this rule:

> Symbolic design patterns only — not diagnosis, measurement, or universal law.

Families are creative categories, not psychological classifications.

## Comedy

### Purpose

Comedy formulas map how jokes, sketches, absurdity, and performance rhythms work.

They are useful for:

- sketch writing
- dialogue writing
- game NPC banter
- satire structures
- absurd escalation
- callback design

### Tone

- fast
- playful
- specific
- absurd
- deadpan-friendly
- sharp without cruelty

### Example formulas

- Giant Hat Disruption Equation
- Deadpan Delta
- Escalation Spiral
- Rule of Three Detonation
- False Importance Theorem
- Professor Phi Comedy Constant

### Boundary style

Comedy should target:

- patterns
- formats
- ego
- bureaucracy
- contradiction
- absurdity
- power systems

Comedy should not target:

- vulnerable people
- grief
- trauma
- disability
- identity-based harm
- real suffering as a cheap punchline

### Family line

> Comedy maps the bit, not the worth of the person.

## Drama

### Purpose

Drama formulas map tension, misunderstanding, conflict, and social escalation.

They are useful for:

- scene design
- relationship arcs
- conflict writing
- group-chat tension
- mystery escalation
- NPC rivalry

### Tone

- theatrical
- observant
- tension-aware
- funny when safe
- careful with pain

### Example formulas

- Overreaction Theorem
- Suspicious Silence Delta
- Grudge Callback Loop
- Specificity Weaponizer
- Professor Phi Drama Constant

### Boundary style

Drama cards can describe escalation without mocking the person experiencing it.

They should include:

- context
- possible repair path
- uncertainty awareness
- non-shaming language

### Family line

> Drama maps the pressure, not a personal defect.

## Sadness / Grief

### Purpose

Sadness and grief formulas map loss, memory, attachment, longing, absence, and witness.

They are useful for:

- reflective prompts
- character arcs
- memory scenes
- grief-sensitive writing
- emotional pacing
- rituals in fiction or games

### Tone

- soft
- spacious
- respectful
- slow
- low-joke density
- witness-first

### Example formulas

- Grief Echo Equation
- Sadness Reservoir
- Nostalgia Time-Glow Equation

### Boundary style

These formulas must never rush grief or imply a deadline.

Preferred language:

- witnessed
- held
- remembered
- given room
- softened over time

Avoid:

- fixed
- solved
- cured
- moved on
- gotten over

### Family line

> Grief has no deadline. The formula is a candle, not a clock.

## Joy / Wonder

### Purpose

Joy and wonder formulas map presence, gratitude, play, awe, hope, relief, beauty, and golden joy.

They are useful for:

- cozy game design
- reward design
- uplifting scenes
- wonder-based learning
- hopeful character arcs
- reflective practices

### Tone

- warm
- bright
- curious
- playful
- humble
- lightly poetic

### Example formulas

- Joy Lift Equation
- Hope Horizon Lift
- Awe Vastness Gate
- Gratitude Gift Lens
- Wonder Golden Joy Spiral

### Boundary style

Joy should never become forced positivity.

Use joy as an invitation, not a demand.

Preferred language:

- notice
- open
- invite
- return
- shimmer
- lift

Avoid:

- just be happy
- choose joy as pressure
- ignore pain
- positive vibes only

### Family line

> Joy is invited, not forced.

## Fire / Boundary

### Purpose

Fire / Boundary formulas map anger, disgust, resentment, protection, violated needs, and clean action.

They are useful for:

- conflict scenes
- boundary arcs
- repair design
- villain-to-repair arcs
- justice themes
- emotional signal mapping

### Tone

- clear
- grounded
- direct
- protective
- repair-oriented

### Example formulas

- Anger Boundary Engine
- Disgust Boundary Chemistry
- Resentment Pressure Ledger

### Boundary style

These formulas should honor anger without glorifying harm.

Preferred language:

- boundary
- signal
- need
- harm
- repair
- clarity
- safety

Avoid:

- revenge math
- domination
- humiliation
- rage as proof of truth
- anger as permission to harm

### Family line

> Fire is a signal. It still needs a safe container.

## Fear / Threat

### Purpose

Fear / Threat formulas map danger, uncertainty, imagination, forecast loops, safety signals, and support.

They are useful for:

- horror design
- survival scenes
- suspense pacing
- reflection prompts
- safety-oriented design
- tension-to-calm arcs

### Tone

- validating
- practical
- grounded
- careful
- safety-first

### Example formulas

- Fear Forecast Loop
- Anxiety Forecast Loop

### Boundary style

Real-world safety comes first.

These formulas should never tell someone fear is irrational by default.

Preferred language:

- known
- imagined
- uncertain
- support
- safety signal
- next step

Avoid:

- just calm down
- nothing is wrong
- irrational
- overreacting
- weak

### Family line

> Fear asks for safety before interpretation.

## Love / Bridge

### Purpose

Love / Bridge formulas map attention, care, trust, compassion, repair, and relationship continuity.

They are useful for:

- relationship arcs
- NPC trust systems
- family healing stories
- mercy-based design
- compassionate bridge systems
- repair mechanics

### Tone

- honest
- warm
- relational
- compassionate
- consent-aware
- repair-oriented

### Example formulas

- Love Bridge Equation
- Compassion Bridge Equation
- Trust Continuity Bridge
- Forgiveness Release Vector

### Boundary style

Love must never be used to excuse control, coercion, or harm.

Forgiveness should never be demanded.

Preferred language:

- consent
- repair
- trust
- truth
- care
- boundary
- presence

Avoid:

- love means tolerating harm
- forgiveness pressure
- spiritual bypassing
- forced reconciliation
- control disguised as care

### Family line

> Love includes truth, repair, and boundaries.

## Calm / Learning

### Purpose

Calm / Learning formulas map safety, grounding, curiosity, agency, confidence, boredom, and motivation.

They are useful for:

- learning systems
- cozy game design
- educational tools
- reflective prompts
- tutorial design
- skill-building arcs

### Tone

- spacious
- clear
- encouraging
- non-school-like
- agency-respecting

### Example formulas

- Calm Grounding Loop
- Curiosity Doorway Formula
- Boredom Agency Drain
- Confidence Evidence Stack
- Motivation Ignition Curve
- Acceptance Landing Field

### Boundary style

Calm should never be used to silence valid anger, grief, or fear.

Learning should not be shame-based.

Preferred language:

- agency
- permission
- curiosity
- small win
- safety
- clarity
- choice

Avoid:

- laziness labels
- shame motivation
- forced calm
- productivity scoring
- school-like punishment language

### Family line

> Learning opens when safety and curiosity have room.

## General Feeling

### Purpose

General Feeling is for experimental or uncategorized formulas that do not yet belong to a stable family.

Use this family sparingly.

### Tone

- exploratory
- humble
- draft-aware
- boundary-forward

### Boundary style

General Feeling formulas should be reviewed carefully before becoming canonical.

They should either move into a clearer family or remain marked as experimental.

### Family line

> Experimental means humble, not less careful.

## Family assignment checklist

When assigning a formula to a family, ask:

1. What pattern is this card mainly mapping?
2. Is the emotional tone sensitive?
3. Which family gives the safest boundary language?
4. What examples would help users understand it?
5. Does the family color/motif support the card?
6. Does the card belong in more than one family?
7. If so, which family is primary and which formulas should be cross-referenced?

## Cross-family bridge examples

Some patterns naturally bridge families:

- Fear ↔ Calm: threat softens through safety and support.
- Anger ↔ Love: boundaries and repair can meet.
- Comedy ↔ Drama: the same structure can become laughter or conflict depending on context.
- Sadness ↔ Joy: memory can carry both ache and gratitude.
- Social Mirror ↔ Love: shame can soften through witness and repair.
- Learning ↔ Wonder: curiosity opens when shame drops.

## Voice lock
