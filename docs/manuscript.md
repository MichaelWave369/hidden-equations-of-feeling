# The Hidden Equations of Feeling

## Structured Abstract

**Context:** Writers, comedians, game designers, NPC/dialogue designers, educators, and reflective creative-tool builders often need memorable ways to reason about emotional patterns without reducing people to clinical scores.

**Objective:** This project presents a playful atlas of symbolic feeling formulas for comedy, drama, sadness, joy, fear, anger, love, shame, calm, and related affective patterns.

**Method:** The formulas are derived through creative synthesis, sketch-comedy structure, narrative design practice, reflective writing, and PHI369 Labs claim-boundary discipline. They are not empirically fitted equations.

**Result:** The atlas defines formula cards with variables, symbolic notation, reflection prompts, example applications, boundaries, misuse warnings, and cross-references. A React app turns the cards into a searchable and interactive symbolic design tool.

**Conclusion:** The Hidden Equations of Feeling is best understood as an affective design-pattern atlas: useful for storytelling, game design, comedy craft, and reflective prompts, while remaining explicit that it is not clinical, diagnostic, or universal.

## Introduction

The project asks a simple playful question: what if feelings, comedy beats, and dramatic spirals could be represented as symbolic equations? Not to measure humans, but to make invisible narrative mechanics easier to see.

Traditional emotion frameworks such as dimensional models, emotion wheels, and appraisal theories offer important scientific and scholarly anchors. This atlas sits beside them as a creative cousin: optimized for writers, designers, educators, and interactive tools rather than empirical measurement.

## Methodology

The equations are symbolic design patterns. Operators such as `+`, `×`, `/`, and `^2` are used as a notation language for stacking, amplification, buffering, and nonlinear spiraling. The formulas were produced through creative synthesis and review-loop refinement, then structured into JSON cards for validation and reuse.

## Claim Boundaries

These formulas are not clinical tools, diagnostic instruments, empirical laws, or claims about universal human psychology. They are culturally situated, draft-stage creative metaphors designed for craft, play, reflection, and humane storytelling.

## Use Cases

- Comedy and sketch writing.
- Drama and character conflict design.
- NPC emotional-state design.
- Reflective journaling prompts.
- Educational discussion about story, emotion, and pattern recognition.
- Interactive creative tools and formula-card games.

## Limitations

Emotions are dynamic, embodied, relational, cultural, and contextual. No static symbolic equation can capture the whole of a human experience. The formulas should be treated as prompts and design lenses, not explanations, predictions, or judgments.

## Future Work

Future versions may expand the full 52-card dataset, add worked examples, publish a stable DOI through Zenodo, add print/export modes, and evaluate usefulness through feedback from writers, game designers, educators, and reflective-tool users.
