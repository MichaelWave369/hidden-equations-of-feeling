# How to Read These Equations

The Hidden Equations of Feeling uses **symbolic design equations**, not empirical mathematics.

The operators are a shared notation language for writers, designers, educators, and playful reflection tools:

- `+` means **stacks with**: separate contributors add pressure, color, or meaning.
- `×` means **amplifies / compounds**: variables intensify each other when they appear together.
- `/` means **buffered by / softened by**: denominator variables reduce, stabilize, contextualize, or repair the pattern.
- `^2` or `3^R` means **nonlinear spiral**: something can run away with itself once repeated or intensified.
- Parentheses show **bundled forces**: a set of variables that act together as one emotional or comic engine.

Example:

```txt
Fear = (Threat × Uncertainty × Imagination²) / (Safety Signals + Knowledge + Support)
```

This does **not** measure fear. It says: fear often grows when threat, uncertainty, and imagination amplify each other, and it may soften when safety signals, knowledge, and support become available.

## Boundary

These equations are creative metaphors and affective design patterns. They are not clinical tools, diagnostic models, empirical laws, or instructions for manipulating people.
